Duckiefloat: a Collision-Tolerant Resource-Constrained Blimp for Long-Term Autonomy in Subterranean Environments

Abstract

There are several challenges for this kind of robotics: maneuverability, perception, autonomy, communica- tion, long-term operation. Inspired by the DARPA Subter- ranean (SubT) Challenge, we look into the case of tunnel envi- ronments. The system is not required to be fully autonomous in the challenge. However, we believe that the robustness of au- tonomy is a key toward long-term operation. Thus we targeted the robots to have fully autonomous ability. The system should also operate continuously for an hour. To operate autonomously without human intervention in such a long period compared to most applications, we consider using an autonomous blimp robot. We use the advantage that airships have low power consumption compared to other aerial vehicles like drones due to the passive floating capability. This is important because search and rescue tasks usually lasts for several days. However, the weight its capable of handling is comparably constrained. Therefore the challenge is to build a system that is both efficient in weight and power consumption and at the same time robust enough to complete the task. In order to reach its long-term capability, a failure-aware algorithm with minimal communication to operator to have situation awareness and send control signals the blimp when needed.
