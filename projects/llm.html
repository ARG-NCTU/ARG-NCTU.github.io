An High-level Task Planner using LLM-Generated Behavior Tree for Aerial Navigation and Manipulation

Ming-Fong Hsieh, Kuan-Ting Yu, Hsueh-Cheng Wang



Abstract

This research delves into the domain of prompt engineering and its empirical application in Large Language Models (LLMs) for complex hierarchical task planning. The study systematically investigates prompt designs in LLMs, with a specific focus on their effectiveness in designing high-level agents. The integration of a simulation tool that combines Gazebo VRX with ChatGPT functionality is also presented, creating a novel VRX-ChatGPT simulation environment to explore ChatGPT’s potential in enabling maritime robotics scenarios, particularly in the realm of USV/UAV navigation. Additionally, the research offers practical examples of positive and negative prompting strategies in the context of aerial manipulation, showcased through the innovative PromptCraft platform for the robotics community. The findings shed light on the empirical aspects of prompt engineering and provide valuable insights for leveraging LLMs in task planning, execution, and designing autonomous agents across various robotic applications.
