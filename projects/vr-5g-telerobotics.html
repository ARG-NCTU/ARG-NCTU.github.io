A Cyber-Secure Long-Distance Virtual Reality-Based Telerobotic System With Private 5G Connectivity

Abstract

This study developed a remote medical service delivery system that empowers medical staff to offer prompt medical assistance and patient care from distant locations, thereby reducing the risk of infection transmission. The system achieves intuitive control for daily dexterous medical tasks through a virtual reality interface, synchronizing hand gestures with robot arm movements and offering semi-autonomous approaches for core and repetitive manipulations. Real-time interaction with patients is facilitated by the latest communication systems, including a private 5G network, ensuring seamless access to medical services over the Internet from any location while safeguarding privacy through data encryption. Demonstrating its potential utility for high-bandwidth, high-precision applications with a compact design, the system enables heterogeneous robots with single or multiple operator modes to provide remote healthcare services akin to those delivered by human staff in a hospital. Moreover, it includes a free-viewpoint platform that demonstrates complex operations from a first-person perspective. The system's usability was confirmed through a comprehensive user study involving a large number of participants, local and global operations, six dexterous tasks, and multiple visualization approaches (N = 80), even for operators without prior experience with robots or the system.
