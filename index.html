Welcome

Dr. Wang is an assistant professor in the Electrical and Computer Engineering and Institute of Electrical and Control Engineering at National Chiao Tung University, Taiwan. Dr. Wang's Assistive Robotics Group focuses on developing robotic systems to solve real-world problems in direct support of indivisuals. Dr. Wang was a postdoctoral associate in the Marine Robotics Group (MRG) and Distributed Robotics Laboratory (DRL) in the Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL), MIT.

Research Fields:

  • Robotics: Autonomous Vehicle, Robotic Vision, Navigation, Manipulation, Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM)
  • Assistive Technology: Blind Navigation, Human Robot Interaction, Sensory Substitution

    Recent Projects

    Robotic guide dog: We develop the robotic guide dog which used a vision-based learning approach to autonomously follow man-made trails on various terrains of general pedestrian environments to enable independent navigation for people who are blind or visually impaired (BVI). Robotic guide dog

    RobotX Competition: We will participate the RobotX Challenge 2018 in Hawaii. RobotX

    Duckietown @ NCTU: We devote to promote autonomy education and research in NCTU and Taiwan. Duckietown @ NCTU

    Wearable Vision System for the Blind: We develop wearable, miniaturized, and energy-efficient robotic systems to provide navigation feedbacks for blind and visually impaired people.

    Previous Projects

    Bocelli Project: We develop wearable, miniaturized, and energy-efficient robotic systems to provide navigation feedbacks for blind and visually impaired people. (Joint work with Profs. Daniela Rus and Anantha Chandrakasan, EECS, MIT)

    JR East Project: We aim at an indoor navigation system using text as landmarks in highly dynamic environments, such as Tokyo subway stations. (Joint work with Prof. John Leonard, Mechanical Engineering, MIT)
