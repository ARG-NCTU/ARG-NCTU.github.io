About

The workshop is initiated by the NERC-MOST joint project: Development of Homogeneous Unmanned Marine Vehicles for Monitoring and Removing Marine Debris by Profs. Mien Van and Hsueh-Cheng Wang The collaborative project synergizes expertise and experiences in modelling and control (UK applicant-Dr. Mien Van) and design system (Taiwanese applicant-Dr. Hsueh-Cheng Wang) to develop an unmanned surface vehicle-linked-unmanned underwater vehicles (USV-UUVs) system for monitoring and cleaning marine debris. This project aims to achieve: (a) technological developments: four core technologies will be developed: (i) design of integrated USV-UUVs system, (ii) dynamic behaviors analysis for the coupled USV-UUVs system, (iii) path planning-based centralized collaborative control to adapt to environmental disturbances, i.e., wind, waves and currents, (iv) integrated Robotic Operating System-Mission Orientated Operating Suite (ROS- MOOS) middleware to facilitate the communication between UUVs and USV, (b) a long term sustainable partnership: Based on the proposed project, a long term sustainable partnership in developing marine robotic technologies for marine applications, i.e., marine robotics for seabed monitoring, military and transportation applications, etc, is built. The research activities of both the UK and Taiwan sites in this project are tightly exchanged and integrated.
